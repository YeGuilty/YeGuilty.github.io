Lore terms and definitions. Orm: The chief god of the Ormite faith. Uhl: A god of the Ormite faith; the smith that created the world and all mortal beings. Zoh: A god of the Ormite faith; the supposed evil god. Heavily associated with azbals and ephsun. Ormadan: A priest of the Ormite faith. Ormadess: A priestess of the Ormite faith. Ormitri: Gender neutral term for the Ormite priest and priestesses. Tarische: a chieftan of a tribesfolk clan. Zohist: A worshipper or follower of Zoh. Azbal: A demon. Azbalette: A female demon. Salaxi: A race of magic-imbued humanoids with glowing red eyes. Pallador: A holy knight of the Maztyon Order, a high-ranking military group serving the Meffacy. Meffacy: The head of the Ormite faith of the realm, lead by the lucifarch. Castle Meffat: The grand fortress of the Meffacy. Also called the "Meffat" for short. Lucifarch: The supreme leader of the Meffacy. Prohedral: Dignitaries of the Meffacy, just below the lucifarch. Ormsbrother: A usually unordained male person who often dedicates most of their life to the Ormite faith. Ormsister: A usually unordained female person who often dedicates most of their life to the Ormite faith. Sisstenrah: The sacred, holy text of the Ormites Brush lion: A certain species of big cat, similar to the real-life mountain lion, but often live in packs and have red fur on their feet. Brush bear: An animal similar to a raccoon and red panda, but bigger and still having a ringed-tail. Diacedes: The god of the monotheistic religion of the faith of Diacedes popular in Jamjahir. Diacedian: Worshippers of Diacedes. Decipheroth: A temple for Diacedians. Diacedium: The sacred, holy text of the Diacedians. Roe: In this lore, it refers to a gold coin used as currency as well, as in: "A gold roe". Reed: As with a roe, this is a silver coin. Dent. As with the roe and reed, this is a copper coin, the lowest value of the three. Ephsun: A mysterious seemingly otherworldly energy manipulated to do extraordinary things; magic. Ephsunic: Pertaining to Ephsun. Amzalt: A red ephsunic crystal used for conjuring or storing ephsun. Ethrol: A flammable purple liquid often used for war and arson. Also called "Jam Fire". Viarphom: A corrosive yellow liquid used for both war, medical, and scientific purposes. Dagg: A wheellock pistol. Crimscayle: A mysterious, rare, red metal of ephsunic origin and having ephsunic properties. Deimovant: A male human ephsun-user. Often relies on using amzalt crystals. Deimovantess: A female human ephsun-user. Often relies on using amzalt crystals. Faugar: A board game common in the realm; similar to the real life ancient game "House of Fortune". Akraphos: The sacred symbol of the Ormite faith. Syrhomil: The accursed symbol of Zoh and sometimes of ephsu itself. Shankila: The sacred symbol of the Diacedian faith. Woods trooper: A brigand, a robber, that often resides in the woods, hiding an ambushing their victims. Inspired by the real-life moss troopers of Scotland. Lacerpel: Monstrous humanoids that prey on humans, having lizard-like skin, sharp teeth, and red eyes. Seroch: A race of holy, higher beings that serve the gods. They are present in both Diacedian and Ormite faiths. Miliconia: The kingdom of the Ormite gods; a place where the faithful are to rest in paradise after death. Azbalyn: The kingdom of Zoh; a place where the sinful are said to be tormented for eternity after death. Also called the Under Reaches. Lagthorp: A monstrous creature that has the bottom half of a four-legged beast and the upper half of human. They are often much larger than humans and make for a formidable opponent. Knot-knower: The spiritual leaders of the tribesfolk. Sometimes they claim to see the future and have the great spirits of the wild speak through them, granting them wisdom. They are called such because they were known to touch the knots of oaks, claiming they stored some of the world's own ancient wisdom. Rakt: The wolf god. Worshipped heavily by the Raktjali tribe. Tavash: The brush lion god. Worshipped heavily by the Tavashani tribe. Hua: The stag god. Worshipped heavily by the Huateshi tribe. Kabernath: The horse god. Worshipped heavily by the Kabernathi tribe. The tribe is assumed to be extict by most. Valda: In the Stangradi empire, a woman who is the supreme religious leader of the Diacedian faith. She is similar to the lucifarch of the Meffacy. Always female and sworn to chastity. Often one of the daughters of the emperor of Stangradis. If he has no daughters, than the position can be given to a cousin, niece, and so forth, going down the bloodline farther from the emperor himself. Leprafegis: Also called blackbreaths, they are giant beasts, similar in shape to the real-life komodo dragon. Their most devastating attack is breathing a thick black caustic smoke that, through prolonged exposure, turns flesh into a dark tar-like substance, one that is preferred to be lapped up by the beast. Hartsword: A greatsword, the blade usually a wide as a human hand, from wrist to the middle-finger's tip. Traditionally, they had quillons in the shape of deer antlers, but now they have a variety of different styles. Warknife: A single-edged sword with a knife-like construction for it's hilt, often possessing a broad blade appropriate for chopping. Long gun: An arquebus-type gun of varying firing mechanisms
